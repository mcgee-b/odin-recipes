Yellow Split Pea Soup

Description

Yellow split pea soup is a thick, comforting dish made by simmering dried split peas with onions, carrots, celery, and broth until everything breaks down into a smooth, hearty base. It develops a deep, earthy flavor as the peas soften, and often gets an extra savory boost from smoked meat like ham or bacon, though it’s just as good made vegetarian. The final result is a warm, filling soup with a velvety texture and simple, nostalgic flavor.

Ingredients

Base

  • 1–2 tablespoons oil or butter
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 2–3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced

Peas & liquid

  • 1½ cups (about 300 g) yellow split peas, rinsed
  • 4–5 cups vegetable or chicken broth

Seasoning

  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Optional but great

  • 1 small smoked ham hock, smoked bacon, or smoked sausage
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • A splash of cream at the end if you want it richer
