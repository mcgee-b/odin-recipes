Deep Dish Pizza

Description

Deep dish pizza is a thick, pan-baked style built with a tall, buttery crust that holds substantial layers. Cheese goes in first, followed by a generous blanket of chunky tomato sauce, creating a dense, structured slice that’s eaten more like a hearty meal than a quick bite.

Ingredients

Crust

  • 3¼ cups all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup yellow cornmeal
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1½ teaspoons salt
  • 2¼ teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 1¼ cups warm water
  • ⅓ cup olive oil
  • ⅓ cup melted butter

Filling

  • 3–4 cups shredded low-moisture mozzarella
  • ½–1 pound Italian sausage
  • Any additional toppings you want

Sauce

  • 1–2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2–3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (28 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • A pinch of sugar if the tomatoes are too acidic
  • Grated parmesan for finishing

Steps

Prepare the dough:

  1. In a bowl, mix warm water, yeast, and sugar. Let it sit until foamy, about 5–10 minutes.
  2. Combine flour, cornmeal, and salt in a large bowl.
  3. Add the yeast mixture, olive oil, and melted butter.
  4. Mix until a rough dough forms, then knead for 5–7 minutes until smooth.
  5. Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover, and let it rise for 1 to 2 hours, until doubled.

Prepare the sauce:

  1. Heat olive oil in a pan over medium.
  2. Add garlic and cook briefly until fragrant.
  3. Stir in crushed tomatoes, oregano, basil, salt, and pepper.
  4. Simmer for 15–20 minutes until slightly thickened. Set aside.

Prep the toppings:

  1. Cook the Italian sausage, if using, breaking it into small pieces.
  2. Slice or prepare any additional toppings.

Shape the crust:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. Lightly oil a deep dish pan or a cast-iron skillet.
  3. Press the dough into the pan, pushing it up the sides to form a tall crust.

Assemble:

  1. Add the cheese first, covering the bottom completely.
  2. Add the cooked sausage and any other toppings.
  3. Spoon the sauce over the top in an even layer.
  4. Sprinkle with parmesan.

Bake:

  1. Bake for 25–30 minutes until the crust is golden and the sauce is bubbling.
  2. Let the pizza rest for 10 minutes before slicing so it holds its shape.
